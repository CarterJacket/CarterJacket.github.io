Rebuilding the core,
without breaking the bank

Harbour Pacific Credit Union runs $4.6B in assets on a 1991 COBOL core that processes everything overnight in batch. Maintenance climbs 12% a year, four developers stand between the bank and a crisis, and younger members are quietly leaving for fintechs. The mandate: modernize the core without losing the staff, the regulator, or the members along the way.

Role: Strategy and analysis, team of three. Team: JACS Of All Trades, with Samantha Ng and Jenise Yang. Format: TechStrat 2026 final round, March 2026.

Situation

A trusted institution with a hidden liability

Harbour Pacific is financially strong and community-rooted: $4.6B in assets, 148,000 members, 9,400 business clients, and $2.8M in annual donations. Its values center on member trust and stewardship, and its balance sheet can comfortably fund a large project with heavy upfront costs. The problem is not money. It is that the technology under the institution has quietly aged into a single point of failure.

Strong foundations

  • $4.6B in assets and a loyal, long-horizon member base
  • Sticky deposits mean reliable returns and capacity for upfront investment
  • Deep community trust and a clear stewardship mission

Underlying values to protect

  • Member trust and decisions made in members' interest
  • Community accountability beyond transactions
  • Responsible, conservative stewardship of member assets
  • Continuous improvement as a stated commitment
Complication

APEX-7 is a clock running down

The core banking system, APEX-7, was installed in 1991 and has had no active development since. It is batch-only, processing roughly 420,000 transactions overnight, and 700 staff across 22 branches depend on it for everything. The risk is concentrated, regulatory, and competitive all at once.

Operational and regulatory risk

  • $4.9M/yr maintenance, rising 12% annually toward a projected $8.7M by year 6
  • Only 4 COBOL developers maintain 2.7M lines of code, one retirement from a crisis
  • 26 security vulnerabilities, 17 unresolved, with a BCFSA review in 12 to 18 months

The competitive bleed

  • No Real-Time Rail, so members use fintechs for instant transfers
  • Mobile app rated 2.3 out of 5, members under 45 drifting away
  • HPCU keeps the mortgage but loses the daily touchpoints that build loyalty
The Key Question

How should Harbour Pacific Credit Union modernize its core banking system, without losing operational stability, regulatory standing, or member trust?

Analysis

Three paths, one credible answer

We weighed the status quo against a full core replacement and a slower phased approach across the criteria the board and the regulator actually care about: regulatory credibility, the legacy support cliff, long-term cost, and member impact. Only a full migration resolves the risk instead of deferring it.

Analyst read: Phasing feels safer but keeps the most dangerous asset alive. A full shift is the only path that both satisfies the regulator and ends the talent-concentration risk.
Vendor Selection

Meridian CoReady fits the values, not just the spec

Three vendors could technically do the migration. The deciding factors were Real-Time Rail readiness, BC migration track record, and where member data physically lives, the last point being central to a credit union built on community trust.

VendorTrack recordReal-Time RailData location
Meridian CoReadyChosen 16 years, 9 migrations, 2 in BC Certified, can launch day 1 Hybrid cloud, Canadian-owned, Toronto HQ
NorthAxis Apex Next 6 years, 3 migrations, 1 in BC Certified, can launch day 1 US HQ, data fully stored in US

Analyst read: Meridian is the only option that pairs day-one Real-Time Rail with Canadian data residency and a proven BC migration record, and it aligns with OSFI B-10 expectations.
Recommendation

The Triple R strategy

Rebuild the core, reclaim the daily relationship, and reinforce trust. The three moves work together: modern infrastructure is wasted if staff resist it, members do not feel it, and the board cannot govern it.

Rebuild

A dual-mode core

Migrate to Meridian and give staff a choice of interface: Core Mode mirrors the familiar APEX-7 workflow for tenured employees, while Smart Mode offers real-time dashboards and AI nudges. Both run through one governed API layer, so Meridian stays the single source of truth with no duplicated data. It removes the COBOL dependency while preserving institutional knowledge.

Reclaim

Beacon, the member app

A real-time, multilingual mobile app that replaces the 2.3-star experience: instant balance updates, unified internal and external transfers, and remittance support for newcomer segments. The goal is to win back the high-frequency daily transactions that build loyalty and reposition HPCU as the member's primary bank, not a secondary one.

Reinforce

A Digital Governance Officer

A new internal executive role that acts as the liaison between the board and the technology team: governing AI boundaries, overseeing migration phase gates, enforcing lifecycle policy, and coordinating regulator communications. An annual Internal Tech Health Assessment keeps governance ahead of regulatory change.
