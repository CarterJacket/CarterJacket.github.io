Charged for change:
powering TransLink's electric future

TransLink approved more than 100 battery-electric buses and a $479M investment, then announced a looming "fiscal cliff." The buses were the easy part. The real problem was that a diesel-era operating model cannot run an energy-constrained fleet. Here is the digital operating plan we built to scale electrification reliably within 18 months.

Situation

From a sustainability pilot to an operating transformation

TransLink is past the experiment stage. Electrification is no longer a green initiative running on the side; it is now the core of how the network plans, dispatches, and maintains its fleet, and it has to happen under real financial pressure. The shift from diesel to electric changes the physics of the operation.

Diesel: built-in slack

  • Refueling is routine and fast, so inefficiencies get absorbed quietly
  • Range is rarely the binding constraint on a daily block
  • Maintenance can stay largely reactive without breaking service

Electric: energy constrained

  • Energy is capped by chargers and grid power, so every inefficiency hits service directly
  • Charging is a capacity system, not a quick top-up
  • Clean BC hydroelectric power is available, but only if depots are retrofitted in time
Complication

The digital stack was built for diesel

TransLink already runs strong systems: CAD/AVL for dispatch, enterprise asset management, employee scheduling, and an analytics function that has delivered $1.8M in cost avoidance. The gap is that none of it was designed for an energy-constrained fleet. Coast Mountain operates at a technology readiness level of 3 to 4 and needs to reach 5 to 6 within 18 months.

Four operating gaps

  • Manual charging scheduling, done by hand as the fleet grows
  • Energy-blind block planning that ignores battery state
  • Availability-based dispatching, not charge-based
  • Fragmented battery diagnostics across the fleet

Why it bites at scale

  • Overnight depot charging becomes a bottleneck as buses scale from 22 to 462
  • A single missed charge cascades into a missed morning pull-out
  • Maintenance is reactive: only 1 in 3 work orders is preventative today
The Key Question

How can TransLink use digital solutions to scale electric bus operations reliably, and reach 50% fleet electrification, within 18 months?

Market Read

Cities that moved fast moved deliberately

The global benchmark set the ambition and the method. Shenzhen runs a fully electric fleet and cut lifetime ownership cost by 36% through disciplined battery rotation. The lesson is that the winning move is operational, not just procurement. We also confirmed that overnight charging is the right window: it is the cheapest, the cleanest on the grid, and it matches the refueling routine depots already run.

Recommendation

Two systems, one fleet

The plan pairs a physical fix with a digital one. Swap the batteries instead of waiting on chargers, then give dispatchers and drivers a single live view so the right bus is always on the right route.

1
Swap, Don't Stop

Rotational battery exchange

When a bus hits 40%, it pulls into the nearest depot, swaps its battery, and returns to route within minutes. Depleted packs charge at the depot while fresh ones go back into service. Swapping decouples bus availability from charger availability, which removes the core constraint and lets the fleet run more routes with fewer buses.

  • 25% reduction in overnight charging demand at depots
  • 90% pull-out success rate within the first year
  • Average battery swap under 15 minutes
2
ChargeLine

Real-time fleet monitoring

One interface for dispatchers and drivers: live battery level per bus, a hub inventory dashboard showing charged packs at each location, automated swap alerts, and maintenance flags surfaced before a fault causes a missed pull-out. Manual coordination across 462 buses is impossible without a centralized view.

  • 95% of drivers using the interface within 6 months
  • 50% reduction in manual dispatch coordination time
  • Shift to 60% preventative maintenance within a year
